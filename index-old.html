    Published Work

    The Cureus Journal of Medical Science

    Abstract

    This study examines the link between university curricula and COVID-19 vaccine hesitancy. An online questionnaire collected demographic data, prior vaccine knowledge, attitudes towards COVID-19 vaccines, and motivational factors for vaccine uptake. Using binary scoring, the degree of hesitancy among students was determined. Exploratory Factor Analysis (EFA) identified three subscales within the Vaccine Hesitancy Survey (VHS): lack of confidence, risk factor concern, and misinformation. Results showed that medical students had higher prior vaccine knowledge and less hesitancy (1.37%) compared to non-medical students (2.55%). The chi-squared test confirmed that vaccine hesitancy is dependent on the curriculum, with non-medical students showing more hesitancy due to lower knowledge and understanding of vaccines.
