LLM Evaluation & Automated Review Systems

Turing · Meta, Apple, Microsoft · 2024–Present

Pod Lead → Calibrator → Tooling Champion → Automated Reviewer architect across three major client streams

+

Problem

Meta needed to improve LLaMA's performance on the SWE-bench benchmark through human annotations. Contributors would "think on behalf of the model"—navigating repositories to trace from a GitHub issue to the patch that resolves it. At 150+ contributors, annotation quality variance directly impacted benchmark scores.

Why It Was Hard

Reasoning through codebases is subjective—what counts as "good" navigation? Pod leads interpreted quality differently. Calibrating 150+ people to consistent standards while maintaining throughput required systems, not just guidelines. Later, scaling to agentic workflows (Agentless SWE-bench) meant building infrastructure that didn't exist.

My Roles (Progressive)

  • Pod Lead (Meta): Managed team of 5 annotators—onboarding, training, daily QA reviews, performance tracking
  • Calibrator (Meta): Reviewed other pod leads' evaluations to ensure cross-team consistency
  • Tooling Champion (Meta): Built infrastructure for Agentless SWE-bench approach, Colab/GCP/Kubernetes systems for agentic annotation workflows
  • Autoreviewer (Apple): Defined quality dimensions, built system prompts per dimension, calibrated AR agents against gold-standard human reviews
  • Autoreviewer (Microsoft — Current): Building AR systems across multiple streams—quality dimension definition, API creation, GCloud VM deployment, deterministic validation scripts, quality & diversity reporting for client delivery

Impact

Annotation quality directly shaped Meta's SWE-bench performance. Promoted through 5 roles in under 8 months. AR system now runs automatically on every trainer submission—storing review history for self-correction, human reviewer validation, and ongoing prompt calibration. Quality reports generated from AR data serve as client deliverables.

What I'd Do Differently

Build calibration tooling earlier. We relied on manual cross-checks too long—an embedding-based similarity check on reasoning traces would have surfaced drift faster.

Agent-Caller Pairing Optimization

Afiniti · 2021–2023

Item Response Theory + diagonal allocation delivering 5-10% revenue lift at sub-100ms latency

+

Problem

Call center routing happens in milliseconds but determines revenue outcomes. The question: which agent should take which caller to maximize conversion? Random routing leaves money on the table. But the "best" pairing depends on latent properties of both agents and callers that you can't directly observe.

Why It Was Hard

Agent skill and caller intent are latent—you only see outcomes. A sale might happen because the agent is great, the caller was easy, or the pairing worked. Disentangling these effects requires careful statistical modeling. And the model has to run in production at sub-100ms latency for real-time routing decisions.

Approach

  • Item Response Theory: Estimated latent agent performance and caller propensity using Bayesian IRT models in Stan—agents and callers as "items" and "respondents"
  • Diagonal Allocation: At runtime, high-expected-revenue callers routed to best-performing agents, low-expected to average—optimal matching under constraints
  • Caller Segmentation: Built caller groups using decision trees; determined split metrics via discriminant analysis to maximize between-group variance
  • Feature Engineering: Engineered real-time features to predict purchase likelihood at call arrival—improved model validation 50%+

Impact

5-10% revenue lift across global telco clients. Led 3 client launches in Iberia region. Promoted DS I → DS II. The diagonal allocation framework became the standard approach for new client deployments.

What I'd Do Differently

Simpler caller segmentation first. We over-engineered the decision tree splits before confirming that finer segmentation actually improved allocation. Often 3-5 groups were enough.

Phase Space Regime Analysis

Markov Chain Finance · Live at markovchainfinance.com

Mapping price dynamics into velocity–acceleration space with Mahalanobis energy for regime classification

+

Problem

Most market tools answer "what happened?" I wanted to answer "what environment are we in?" Most trading days are statistically unremarkable—they cluster near the origin in phase space. The question is detecting when they aren't, and giving investors context instead of predictions.

Why It Was Hard

Phase space requires estimating covariance structure, not just volatility. Choosing the right energy metric matters—Mahalanobis distance accounts for correlation between velocity and acceleration that simpler measures miss. Distinguishing genuine regime transitions from noise, and making the output actionable rather than academic, were the real engineering challenges.

Approach

  • Phase Space Mapping: Returns as velocity, change in momentum as acceleration—each day becomes a coordinate in 2D phase space
  • Mahalanobis Energy: E(t) = xᵀΣ⁻¹x measures distance from equilibrium, accounting for covariance structure
  • Markov Transitions: 1st-order transition matrix plus 2nd-order tensor (previous regime conditions the next)
  • Energy-Conditioned Splits: Separate transition behavior inside vs outside the 90% stability ellipse
  • Survival Analysis: Kaplan–Meier curves for regime duration with current dwell marker

Output

Live at markovchainfinance.com. Validated on BTC-USD with 3,653 days of data. Three tools in production: Phase Regime analysis, Regime Detector with LLM explanations, and Portfolio Optimizer with diversification metrics.

What I'd Do Differently

Covariance window sensitivity matters more than model complexity. I spent time on elaborate regime definitions before discovering that the rolling window for estimating Σ was the dominant factor in output stability.

Markov Chain Finance

Decision-Support Research Platform

"Reducing emotionally amplified mistakes beats maximizing returns. Show people where they are, not what will happen."

A decision-support platform for self-directed investors and quantitative practitioners. The core philosophy is context over prediction—showing what kind of environment you're operating in rather than forecasting what happens next. Not alpha generation. Decision quality under uncertainty.

The Framework

Phase space analysis applied to financial assets. Price is mapped into velocity (returns) and acceleration (change in momentum). Each trading day becomes a point in phase space—most cluster near the origin, statistically unremarkable. Distance from equilibrium is measured using Mahalanobis energy E(t) = xᵀΣ⁻¹x. Breaching the 90% stability contour signals a statistically rare state.

Live Tools

  • Phase Regime: Map any ticker into velocity–acceleration phase space. Four quadrants (Accelerating Up, Decelerating Down, Accelerating Down, Decelerating Up). 1st-order Markov transitions, 2nd-order transition tensor, energy-conditioned splits inside vs outside the 90% stability ellipse, Kaplan–Meier survival curves per regime, forward return distributions
  • Regime Detector: Volatility state (Low / Medium / High via 20-day rolling std percentile) + trend state (Strong / Weak / Negative via 50d vs 200d SMA). Transition probability matrix, historical regime comparisons, LLM-powered decision framing. Supported assets: SPY, QQQ, BTC-USD, ETH-USD, GLD
  • Portfolio Optimizer: Effective number of bets (PCA-based diversification measure), Mean-Variance (MPT) max-Sharpe weights, Risk Parity equal-risk-contribution weights, drawdown analysis, Sortino ratio comparison across strategies

Research Layer

  • Factor-Level Phase Space: Rolling PCA on an 8-asset universe extracts three factors—PC1 Market, PC2 Rotation, PC3 Idiosyncratic—each mapped independently into phase space
  • Lead/Lag Discovery: PC1 regime transitions lead asset-level transitions by approximately 13 days
  • Cross-Factor Conflict: A conflict score measuring when factors disagree—high conflict degrades transition predictability. Conflict-conditioned transition matrices show regime persistence changes under high vs low conflict
  • Rebalancing: Energy-triggered rebalancing with a low-conflict filter achieves Sharpe 1.48 vs calendar monthly at 1.44
  • Stress-Strain Analysis: Drawdown event detection (peak → trough → recovery) with stress–strain curve fitting across assets
